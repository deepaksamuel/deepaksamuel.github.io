About TIPP 2026

Please note that this is only a test page and nothing in this page should be deemed official. The official page will be available soon.

The TIPP conference series, the science driven cross-disciplinary conference, started in Tsukuba, Japan in 2009 (TIPP 2009), followed by Chicago in 2011 (TIPP 2011), Amsterdam in 2014 (TIPP2014), Beijing in 2017 (TIPP2017), Canada in 2021 (TIPP2021) and South Africa in 2023 (TIPP2023). The conference aim is to provide a stimulating atmosphere for scientists and engineers from around the world. The 2021 edition will take place virtually. The conference will, as in the past, include plenary invited talks and parallel tracks with contributions outlining state-of-the-art developments in different areas. The program focus is on all areas of detector development and instrumentation in particle physics, astro-particle physics and closely related fields, in particular:

Conference Focus Areas

  • Accelerator-based particle physics
  • Non-accelerator particle physics and particle astrophysics
  • Experiments with synchrotron radiation and neutrons
  • Nuclear physics
  • Cosmology
  • Instrumentation and monitoring of particle and photon beams
  • Applications in photon science, biology, medicine, and engineering

Venue Information

Tata Institute of Fundamental Research (TIFR)

TIFR is located near the southernmost tip of Mumbai. Known formerly as Bombay, the city is the financial and commercial nerve centre of India. It is conveniently connected by air to other cities around the world.

TIFR is a premier institution of the country under the umbrella of the Department of Atomic Energy, as well as a deemed University awarding degrees for master's and doctoral programs. The Institute was founded in 1945 with support from the Sir Dorabji Tata Trust under the vision of Dr. Homi Bhabha. The Department of High Energy Physics (DHEP) at TIFR is a leading group in the Belle, CMS, and INO experimental activities within the country.

The Homi Bhabha Auditorium can house about 1200 participants will be used for the plenary sessions, the lecture theatres of small to moderate sizes will be used for parallel sessions.

Visa

Valid visa is required to enter India for all nationalities. Visa can be obtained from the nearest Indian Consulate/Embassy to you. Upon registration, a letter of invitation will be sent to you that may be used for VISA application. Please let us know by email if you face any difficulty in the application process.

Accomodation and travel

There are several 3 to 4 star hotels with varied price range around the proposed conference venue. To name a few, Astoria,Chateau Windsor, Fariyas, Vivanta by Taj President, Taj Mahal Palace and many more. The typical per night cost varies anywhere from ₹ 3000 to 9000 (aprox. $ 35 to 110). We have negotiate the best possible rates with a selected number of hotels for details click here . We shall be able to accommodate needy conference delegates in a number of cheaper options that include the institute guesthouse, YMCA and YWCA. The typical per night cost would be ₹ 1000 - 4000.

Airports

The International Airport of Mumbai is at Sahar which is about 25 km from the institute. The domestic airport is about 20 km. Pre-paid (fixed fare) taxi service (special stand in the Airport) is also available (approximate fare ₹ 800 from Sahar and ₹ 600 from Santacruz). We expect the delegates to take either prepaid taxi from the airport or arrange for pick-up from the respective hotels. In case someone is arriving very late in night (between mid-night till 5AM) or feels uncomfortable travelling in the prepaid taxi, we can arrange for pickup upon prior request. Please provide flight details for the logistic purpose via email

Railway

CST (Chatrapati Shivaji Terminus, formerly known as Victoria Terminus or VT) and Mumbai Central are the nearest railway heads on central and western railways. Taxi service is available from both these stations throughout the day and night. Approx. fare from CST (VT) to the institute is ₹ 150 and from Mumbai Central it will be ₹ 200 or so.

Local Transport

Mumbai is well connected by a network of metro, bus, rail and taxi servces.

Weather

December to February is the beginning of the best season of the year and is generally dry with day/night temperatures between 15-25 °C. The northern parts of the country become much cooler with the temperature going below 5 °C during the night.

Safety

Mumbai is a 'mega city' and one of the largest metropolitan regions in India. It is the financial, commercial and entertainment capital of the country. Thanks to its liberal cosmopolitan nature, Mumbai has attracted large number of migrants from India as well as from abroad belonging to diverse social and religious groups. It is truly a melting pot of all cultures. It may be noted that like many other Asian countries India is a conservative country. In particular, public displays of affection are frowned upon for couples and may garner an unwelcome attention. Homosexuality is technically illegal. Despite that, there are active LGBT communities in India and Mumbai in particular. For more details, please see: http://www.lonelyplanet.com/india/gay-and-lesbian-travellers. As in any other large and crowded city there are opportunistic thieves in operation but not at an alarmingly high rate. Please take care of your belongings and do not wander into seemingly deserted areas late at night.

Tourism

Maharashtra attracts tourists from other Indian states and foreign countries. It was the second most visited Indian state by foreigners and fifth most visited state by domestic tourists in the country in 2024.
